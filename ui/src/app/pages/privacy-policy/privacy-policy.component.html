Privacy Policy – EOSC Innovation Sandbox

1. Introduction

The EOSC Innovation Sandbox serves as a testing and integration environment for various services provided by independent service providers. This platform allows users to access, test, and interact with a range of experimental and production-ready services.

Each service available through the sandbox is managed by its respective provider, who remains responsible for the collection, processing, and protection of personal data related to the use of their services. This Privacy Policy acts as an overarching framework and refers users to the specific privacy policies of each service provider.

2. Scope of this Policy

This Privacy Policy applies exclusively to the general operation of the EOSC Innovation Sandbox and does not govern the personal data processing activities of individual services accessible through the platform.

Users should be aware that:

  • he sandbox itself does not require user registration, authentication, or the direct collection of personal data.
  • All data processing activities are conducted exclusively by the service providers offering services within the sandbox.
  • Each service provider is responsible for its own privacy practices, terms of use, and data protection measures.

3. Data Collection by the Sandbox

Since the EOSC Innovation Sandbox is publicly accessible and does not require authentication, it does not directly collect any personal data from users.

The platform may collect anonymised technical and operational data strictly for operational, analytical, and security purposes. This data includes, but is not limited to:

  • Technical Metadata: Aggregate information on platform usage such as page visits, traffic volume, session duration, and performance metrics.
  • Error Logs: Details related to system errors, failed requests, and service malfunctions, used for diagnostics and maintenance.
  • Device and Browser Information: Anonymised data about user devices, operating systems, browser types, screen resolutions, and system configurations to optimise compatibility and performance.
  • Network Information: Generalised geolocation based on IP range (not linked to individual users), referring URLs, and anonymised network usage statistics.
  • Interaction Data: Non-identifiable patterns of how users interact with the interface (e.g., clicks, scroll depth, or navigation flows), used to improve usability and functionality.

This data is not linked to any identifiable individuals and is used solely to ensure the functionality, usability, performance, and security of the platform. All data is processed in a manner that preserves user anonymity and complies with applicable data protection regulations.

4. Third-Party Services and Privacy Policies

All services accessed through the EOSC Innovation Sandbox are provided by independent service providers, each of which is responsible for its own data collection and processing. These services may require user registration, authentication, or personal data collection based on their operational needs.

Before accessing a third-party service, users should carefully review the respective provider’s privacy policy, which will outline:

  • The types of data collected by the service.
  • The legal basis for data processing.
  • User rights regarding data access, correction, or deletion.
  • Data security measures employed by the provider.

Links to the relevant privacy policies will be provided alongside each service within the sandbox environment.

5. User Responsibilities

As the EOSC Innovation Sandbox is a public environment, users are responsible for:

  • Reviewing the privacy policies of any services they access through the platform.
  • Ensuring compliance with the individual terms of service and privacy guidelines of each provider.
  • Using the platform responsibly and respecting the terms set by third-party service providers.

6. Security Measures

While the sandbox does not process personal data directly, the platform maintains standard security protocols to ensure system stability and operational integrity. These measures include:

  • Regular system monitoring and maintenance.
  • Protection against unauthorised access or misuse of the platform’s infrastructure.
  • Secure hosting and network safeguards.
